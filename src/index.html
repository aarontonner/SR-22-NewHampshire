understanding SR22 Insurance in New Hampshire

  • SR22 insurance in New Hampshire is a certificate of financial responsibility required for drivers who have committed certain offenses, such as DUI, multiple traffic violations, or driving without insurance.   • SR-22 insurance in New Hampshire is not a separate insurance policy but an add-on to your existing policy. It serves as proof to the state that you have the necessary liability coverage. This requirement is often imposed on high-risk drivers as a means of ensuring they carry sufficient insurance. The process involves your insurance provider filing an SR-22 form with the New Hampshire Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) on your behalf, which confirms that you are adequately insured.

New Hampshire SR22 Insurance Requirements

In New Hampshire, SR-22 insurance requirements mandate that you carry minimum liability coverage of $25,000 per person for bodily injury, $50,000 per accident for bodily injury, and $25,000 for property damage. These limits ensure that you are financially responsible in the event of an accident. The SR-22 filing period typically lasts three years, during which it's essential to maintain continuous coverage without any lapses.
